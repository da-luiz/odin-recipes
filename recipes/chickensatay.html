Chicken Satay

Chicken satay is a popular Southeast Asian dish consisting of marinated and skewered chicken pieces grilled or barbecued to perfection. The chicken is usually served with a savory peanut sauce and often accompanied by cucumber slices, onions, and rice or flatbread. Here's a simple recipe for making delicious chicken satay at home:


Ingredients:
For Chicken Satay:
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (optional, for extra flavor)
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes

For Peanut sauce:
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for a spicy kick)
  • Water (to adjust the sauce consistency)

Steps:
  1. In a bowl, prepare the marinade for the chicken. Combine soy sauce, vegetable oil, curry powder, turmeric powder, brown sugar, and fish sauce (if using). Mix well.
  2. Add the sliced chicken to the marinade and toss to coat the chicken pieces evenly. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes (or longer for more flavor)
  3. While the chicken is marinating, prepare the peanut sauce. In a saucepan, combine peanut butter, coconut milk, soy sauce, brown sugar, lime juice, grated ginger, minced garlic, and cayenne pepper (if using). Stir over low heat until the sauce is smooth and well combined. If the sauce is too thick, you can add water to achieve your desired consistency. Set aside.
  4. After marinating, thread the chicken slices onto the soaked wooden skewers.
  5. Preheat your grill, grill pan, or barbecue to medium-high heat. Lightly grease the surface to prevent sticking.
  6. Grill the chicken satay skewers for about 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are fully cooked and have beautiful grill marks.
  7. Once the chicken satay is cooked, remove them from the grill and serve hot with the prepared peanut sauce.
  8. Garnish with sliced cucumbers and onions if desired. You can also serve it with steamed rice or flatbread for a complete and satisfying meal.

Enjoy your delicious and flavorful chicken satay with peanut sauce! It's perfect for parties, gatherings, or as a delightful appetizer.
